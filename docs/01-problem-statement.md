# Problem Statement

Computational systems increasingly generate, transform, retrieve, summarize and reason over information originating from many independent sources.

Although provenance records where information came from, provenance alone does not determine whether a resulting claim should be trusted.

The central research question is:

> Given a computational claim and its complete evidence chain, how should a system estimate trust in a mathematically rigorous, reproducible and explainable way?

A successful theory should distinguish between:

- source reliability
- evidence relevance
- evidence independence
- provenance completeness
- uncertainty
- contradiction
- temporal freshness
- model calibration

The long-term objective is to develop a general theory that applies across AI, scientific communication, software ecosystems and digital knowledge systems.
