# Computational Trust Theory

Computational Trust Theory (CTT) is a research initiative exploring the mathematical foundations of trust in computational systems.

Rather than treating trust as a single score, CTT investigates how trust emerges from evidence, provenance, uncertainty, contradiction, source quality and inference.

## Motivation

Modern AI systems increasingly generate claims from retrieved documents, datasets, knowledge graphs, software, sensors and prior model outputs. Existing systems frequently record provenance but provenance alone is insufficient to determine whether a claim should be trusted.

A source may be authoritative but outdated. Evidence may be relevant but incomplete. Multiple sources may agree because they share the same origin rather than providing independent confirmation. Models may express high confidence while being poorly calibrated.

CTT asks a more fundamental question:

> What mathematical properties should a computational trust system satisfy?

## Vision

Develop a general mathematical theory that can support:

- AI systems
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- Knowledge graphs
- Scientific publishing
- Software supply chains
- Digital archives
- Sensor networks
- Future computational systems

## Research Layers

1. Mathematical theory
2. Trust algebra
3. Trust graph model
4. Formal specifications
5. Reference implementations
6. Benchmarks and datasets

## Status

This repository is in the research phase. The current objective is to define the problem, terminology, assumptions, and research questions before proposing algorithms or standards.
